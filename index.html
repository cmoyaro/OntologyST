Ontology Specification Draft
The Smart Tourism Ontology represents the key concepts and relationships involved in the management and promotion of smart tourism ecosystems. It covers entities such as hotels, attractions, restaurants, transport systems, and events, facilitating their semantic connection with cities and travelers through linked data principles.
This ontology has been developed to semantically represent the domain of smart tourism. It covers the main entities such as hotels, attractions, events, restaurants, and transport options, all connected through cities. The Smart Tourism Ontology enables the integration, interoperability, and sharing of tourism-related information within intelligent digital ecosystems, improving the accessibility and reuse of data across platforms and services.

EventToFestivalback to ToC or SWRL ToC

If an event has the type "Festival", it is classified as a Festival.

Event c(?) , type dp(?,?) , stringEqualIgnoreCase(?) -> Festival c(?)

ExpensiveRestaurantback to ToC or SWRL ToC

If a restaurant has an average price greater than 50, it is considered expensive.

Restaurant c(?) , averagePrice dp(?,?) , greaterThan(?) -> Restaurant c(?)

HotelToLuxuryback to ToC or SWRL ToC

If a hotel has more than 4 stars, it is classified as Luxury.

Hotel c(?h) , stars dp(?h,?s) , greaterThan(?) -> Luxury c(?h)
